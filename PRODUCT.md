# Product

## Register

product

## Users

IELTS candidates who already know how to practice but underperform when the exam becomes timed, irreversible, and socially uncomfortable. They use the product alone, often at a desk before or after school, work, or tutoring, with a focused but anxious mindset.

## Product Purpose

IELTS Pressure Lab trains exam behavior under controlled stress. It simulates the parts of the IELTS experience that normal study apps soften: vanishing reading passages, no-rewind listening, no-delete writing, cold-start speaking, and full-section transitions.

## Brand Personality

Disciplined, clinical, and exacting. The product should feel like a serious training instrument, not a cozy course marketplace or motivational study app.

## Anti-references

Avoid social leaderboards, fake encouragement, soft tutoring mascots, generic dark SaaS hero pages, purple-blue gradients, decorative glass cards, and IELTS marketing pages that sell confidence without pressure mechanics.

## Design Principles

- Pressure is the product: every major surface should expose a constraint, timer, lock, or recovery state.
- Neutral beats motivational: use factual copy and telemetry instead of praise.
- Show the drill, not the pitch: the first screen should be usable product UI, not a landing page.
- Personal progression only: badges and unlocks are self-measurement, never social comparison.
- Familiar controls, unfamiliar intensity: controls should feel standard so the pressure comes from exam rules, not confusing UI.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, visible keyboard focus, touch targets of at least 44px, reduced-motion alternatives, and color states paired with text/icon labels.
